Impetigo and Ecthyma

Impetigo is a superficial skin infection that rarely causes systemic
symptoms. Ecthyma is a skin infection characterized by crusted sores
beneath which ulcers form. It is a deep form of impetigo, as the same
bacteria causing the infection are involved. Ecthyma causes deeper
erosions of the skin into the dermis.

Gram stain and culture of the pus or exudates from skin lesions of
impetigo and ecthyma are recommended to help identify whether
Staphylococcus aureus and/or beta-hemolytic Streptococcus is the cause.

Bullous and non-bullous impetigo can be treated with oral or topical
antimicrobials, but oral therapy is recommended for patients with
numerous lesions or in outbreaks affecting several people to help
decrease transmission of infection. Treatment for ecthyma should be an
oral antimicrobial.

Treatment for impetigo with few lesions

  • Mupirocin ointment to affected area q8h for 5 days ($) [M]

Treatment for ecthyma and for impetigo with numerous lesions or
whose symptoms have not responded to topical treatment
Treat orally for 7 days
  • Cephalexin 250 mg PO q6h ($) [R]
    • OR
  • Dicloxacillin 250 mg PO q6h ($) [DI]

Alternative for penicillin allergy >
  • Doxycycline 100MG PO q12h ($) [DI]
IF MRSA confirmed or suspected:
  • Trimethoprim sulfamethoxazole 1 DS tablet PO q12h ($) [R,DI]
    • OR
  • Doxycycline 100MG PO q12h ($) [DI]
